Personal Project

ðŸ“± TikTok Channel

The Challenge

Before moving to Australia for university, I was quite shy and struggled with self-confidence. The turning point came when I saw other Vietnamese students who had become influencers by sharing their experiences abroad. Inspired by their success, I decided to give content creation a go.

Early Struggles

Initially, recording videos in public felt very uncomfortable, so I stuck to POV content, capturing moments around Wollongong and Sydney with background music. Despite my effort, I soon noticed that my approach wasn't attracting much audience engagement. It became clear I needed to change my strategy.

Breaking Through Barriers

I then started using voiceovers, gradually building up to recording myself on camera. Each step pushed me further outside my comfort zone, and I had to overcome my fear of being seen and heard. This process challenged me both personally and creatively. Over time, I became much more at ease with sharing my authentic self online.

"The TikTok channel has completely changed me. I am now far more confident and genuinely enjoy connecting with people both online and in person."

The Transformation

This experience in content creation not only led to personal growth but has also enhanced my ability to communicate and build meaningful relationships in Australia. What started as a personal challenge became a catalyst for developing the confidence and communication skills that now define who I am.

Real Example: The Internship Challenge

In my second year, I began searching for internship opportunities. As someone new to the local job market, I started cold-emailing recruiters and applying through job websites, confident that my polished resume, cover letter and portfolio would attract attention.

However, responses were scarce and, when they did arrive, they were rejections. Initially, I felt disappointed, but further research revealed my applications were likely lost among hundreds of others.

Realising I needed a different approach to stand out, I explored new options. In my second semester, I enrolled in BCM 314, an internship subject. Thanks to a recommendation from my tutor, I secured my first placement with Sample Assist.

Building on this experience, I later sought another internship for further development, reaching out to the Vietnamese student association in NSW and successfully obtaining a referral for a new internship.

This journey taught me the value of staying positive, adapting to obstacles, and taking proactive steps when faced with challenges.
